Mets Beat A's, 2‐0, Lead World Series by 3 Games to 2

New York ended its three‐day involvement with the 70th World Series without riot or ruin last night as the Mets defeated the Oakland A’s, 2‐0, and took the Series lead for the first time.

They sent the crowd of 54,817 persons into chants and cheers for the three‐hit pitching of Jerry Koosman and Tug McGraw and for the glovemen behind them. And at the height of McGraw’s latest adventure as an escape artist with the bases loaded, Mayor Lindsay stood in the front‐row box nearest the home dugout and held high a 5‐foot sign that ecboed the team’s war cry:

“Ya Gotta Believe.”

It was the sixth time in the last two weeks that the Mayor had watched the Mets do their thing in playoff and Series, and a strange thing it is. They went from last place to the pennant in six weeks, fell behind the defending champion A’s twice in the Series and finally passed them last night, to move within one game of the championship.

Nobel for U.S. Economist; Australian Writer Chosen

STOCKHOLM, Oct. 18—Wassily Leontief, the Russian‐born Harvard economist, and Patrick White, the Australian novelist, won Nobel honors today.

Professor Leontief, director of Harvard University’s Economic Research Project, won the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Science for 1973. The professor, who is 67, was chosen by the Royal Swedish Academy of Science for his development of the so‐called «inputoutput» method of economic analysis, used in various forms in more than 50 industrialized countries for planning and forecasting.

Each winner will receive a cash award equivalent to about $121,00
